February first.

The 2026 transformation initiative has been postponed indefinitely. It was in an email this morning, two sentences buried in a project status update. "Given current priorities, the transformation workstream will be paused pending Q2 resource alignment." Resource alignment means nobody has time. Q2 means maybe never.

My coworker walked past my desk at eight-forty-five. No mention of the year being theirs. No update on Portuguese or the morning routine. They had the expression of someone who remembered why things were the way they were. I recognized it. I wore it for a long time before I stopped noticing.

The vision email is still in my folder. The initiatives it announced are not active. The goals it described are not being measured. The urgency it performed has dissolved into the first weeks of the quarter without anyone calling a meeting to discuss it.

This is not failure. It's the system working as designed. The vision email exists to mark the start of something. What happens after the start is a different process entirely, one that runs on headcount, budget, and actual priorities, none of which appeared in the email.

I've watched this cycle enough times to stop counting how many initiatives are in my archive folder, announced and then absorbed back into the noise of ordinary operations.

Eighteen years. Every February first looks the same.

The inbox is full. The coffee is hot. The transformation is postponed.

We'll begin again in January.
