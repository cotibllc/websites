Closed the job search tabs this morning.

Not dramatically. I didn't decide to close them. I just noticed they'd been sitting there for two weeks without me opening them, and I closed them the way you close something you've already moved on from without realizing it.

Here's what I figured out somewhere in the middle of March: the problem isn't this company. The problem is the category. I was operating on the assumption that my company's specific dysfunction, the budget math, the vision emails, the calibration sessions, was a feature of this place. That somewhere else had solved it. That there was a company with honest performance reviews and headcount that matched workload and leadership that said what it meant.

I interviewed at one of those companies. Their dysfunction was just newer. Same emails with different branding. Same math with a different spreadsheet. Same meetings with different slides. The grass is the same grass. It's just a different shade of brown depending on which side you're standing on.

I'm fifty. I have a mortgage, two daughters in high school, eighteen years of institutional knowledge that's only worth something here. The market looked at my resume and saw a liability. I looked at the market and saw a mirror.

So I'm staying.

Not because I've made peace with the dysfunction. Not because the budget math got better or the calibration session got fairer or the vision email got truer. I'm staying because leaving requires a destination, and I couldn't find one that was actually different from where I started.

This is not resignation in the emotional sense. It's more like clarity. I understand the system now in a way that requires no further investigation. It's the same system everywhere. You can transfer your location within it. You can't leave it.

The backlog is at sixty-three. I'm going to work through it.

That's the job.
