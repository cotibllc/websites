There was a leadership offsite this week.

I was not invited. This is not unusual. I've been here eighteen years and attended zero offsites. I've received fourteen summary emails. The ratio feels about right.

The subject line was "Key Takeaways." It arrived Friday at 4:47 PM. Seven bullet points under a header that said "Key Takeaways from Our Leadership Alignment Session." I read them twice and counted the ones that contained a specific action, a responsible party, or a deadline.

The count was zero.

I'm documenting them here for the record. We are committed to a culture of accountability. Communication will be a priority going forward. We will continue to invest in our people. Alignment across teams remains essential. We are excited about where we are headed as an organization. More details to follow. Thank you to everyone who participated.

Bullet point seven thanked people for participating in an event that no one outside the offsite was invited to participate in.

A takeaway is something you take away. It implies movement. A thing that was in one place and is now in another. None of the seven bullet points moved anything. They described existing conditions. They expressed orientation toward future conditions. They did not specify what would change, when, or who would change it.

The subject line said Key Takeaways. The email contained no takeaways.

This is not a complaint. It is an observation.

I still have all fourteen emails. They live in a folder called Offsite Summaries. Three of them mentioned IT specifically. Two said "technology will be a key enabler." One said "we will be reviewing our systems infrastructure." No one followed up after any of the three.

The bullet points change slightly each year. The structure hasn't changed once in eighteen years.

More details to follow.
