Two people left my team last week.

Sarah was here eleven years. She took the severance package and I don't blame her. Marcus got an offer somewhere else and left on a Tuesday. He didn't say goodbye. He just stopped showing up, and then his laptop was in IT waiting to be wiped.

Their desks are still there. The equipment is still there. The forty-three items in the ticket queue that belonged to both of them are still there, now distributed across three people instead of five.

My manager called a team meeting. Subject line: "Path Forward." I noticed the same subject line from the budget email. I don't think anyone intended that.

He asked how we'd absorb the workload.

I told him we wouldn't. We'd do less. Slower. With more errors. Things would break and we'd fix them eventually. Projects would miss deadlines and we'd explain why. He asked what I meant by that.

I meant the math. Five people to three people is not a transition. It's a reduction. The work doesn't adjust because the team did. The work stays the same and the time available to do it shrinks, and at some point the backlog stops being a backlog and starts being a permanent condition.

He said this was a transition period.

I've heard "transition period" before. It usually means this is the new normal and we're waiting for everyone to stop noticing.

I updated my resume that afternoon. Not because I know where I'm going. Because the math says staying isn't free either, and I want options that are at least theoretically available.

The backlog is at sixty-three items now.

I'm going to keep updating the number.
