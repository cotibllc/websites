Budget email came through this morning.

Subject line: "Important Update on Our Path Forward."

I've read this email before. Not a version of it. This email. The language is the same because the language is always the same. Cost optimization. Travel freeze. Hiring halt. Discretionary spending under review. We're committed to investing in our people, it says, two sentences after announcing the hiring freeze.

I've been here long enough to know what "path forward" means. It means the path is narrowing. The word "forward" is doing structural work in that sentence. It points you toward a future that looks better than the present, which is useful when the present involves a headcount reduction.

The record is worth stating clearly: Q4 revenue up twenty-three percent. Best year in a decade. The all-hands deck said that. The CEO said it twice, and then said it again when people didn't react with enough enthusiasm. Record year.

And then the email.

I'm not surprised. I've stopped expecting alignment between what the company earns and what the company spends on the people who helped it earn it. Those are two separate conversations happening in two separate rooms, and the rooms don't communicate.

What I know from reading the path forward emails over the years is that the path is always forward, the timeline is always soon, and the specifics arrive later in a follow-up that everyone has mostly stopped paying attention to by the time it lands.

I filed this one with the others.

The path forward looks a lot like the path we've already been on.
