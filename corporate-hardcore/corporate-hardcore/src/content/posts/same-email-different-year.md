Got the company's 2026 vision email this morning.

Checked it against 2025.

Same goals. Same initiatives. Same buzzwords. Different font.

2025 was the year of transformation. So was 2024. And 2023. We transform annually. The word has stopped meaning anything. It's just the thing you say in January before the calendar takes over and everything goes back to what it was.

I keep them all. Seven years of vision emails, filed in a folder I labeled "Vision Emails" because I had a feeling I'd need to refer back. The VP of Strategy updates the year in the header. That's the only edit. I checked. Innovation, agility, customer obsession. Same three words, same order, seven years running.

In 2019, we prioritized innovation. We're still working on the same project from that announcement. Seven years of declared innovation. The project is in its fourth relaunch.

There's a line at the bottom of every email, word for word across all seven years: "This year will be different."

I don't think anyone who writes that line believes it. I don't think they disbelieve it either. It exists in a category beyond belief, like the "your call is very important to us" message you hear on hold. The words are present. Their relationship to truth is beside the point.

I filed the 2026 email in the folder. Twelve months from now I'll pull it out and put it next to the 2027 version.

It'll be the same. It always is.
