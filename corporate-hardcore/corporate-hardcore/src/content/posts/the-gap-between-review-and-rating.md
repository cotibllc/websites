# The Gap Between the Review and the Rating

*Performance Review Arc — Video 2*

---

My manager opened his laptop and started reading from a document I'd never seen.

"Consistently meets expectations." Pause. "Strong technical contributor." Another pause. "Demonstrates reliability and institutional knowledge."

I noticed two things. First, he was reading, not talking. The words were already written. The document existed before we sat down. Second, I could see the reflection of his screen in his glasses. He wasn't looking at notes about my performance. He was looking at the final form.

The review wasn't a conversation. It was a delivery mechanism.

He got to the rating. "You're a three this year. Solid three."

I'd been a solid three for six years. I asked what it would take to be a four.

He said I was doing great work. He said the team really values my experience. He said the rating system is complex, and there are a lot of factors.

He didn't answer the question.

I've learned to hear what isn't said in those meetings. "You're doing great" means "we're not firing you." "The team values you" means "you're useful." "The rating system is complex" means "it's not about you, and also, it is about you."

What he meant was: the decision was made before you walked in. The review is the paperwork. The rating was set in a room you weren't in, by people who weren't in your review, based on a budget number that existed before anyone looked at what you did this year.

I exceed expectations and earn less than last year.

That tracks.

The review isn't the evaluation. The calibration session is the evaluation. The review is just how they tell you what they already decided.
