I've been applying for two weeks.

Twelve positions. All in my field. All within my skill set. All with job descriptions I could have written myself, because I've been doing this work for eighteen years and I know what it requires.

The postings want five to seven years of experience. I have eighteen. I didn't understand why that was a problem until I thought about it from the other direction. Too senior means too expensive. Too experienced means too likely to have opinions. Too much history means you've watched enough initiatives fail to know which ones are already failing before they're announced.

I tried removing the early years from my resume. Make it look like I started in 2015 instead of 2007. The gap disappears. The salary expectations implied by eighteen years don't.

Three rejections. Form emails. "We've decided to move forward with other candidates whose experience more closely aligns with our current needs." I've read that sentence enough to decode it. "Current needs" means younger. "More closely aligns" means cheaper.

Nine silences. Those are the ones that bother me more. The rejection at least acknowledges you existed in the process. The silence means you were filtered before a person read your name.

The job postings use words like "digital native," "passionate disruptors," "high-energy self-starters." I've been reading corporate language for eighteen years. I know what those phrases are doing. They're not describing job requirements. They're describing an age range.

I'm competent. Competent doesn't disrupt. Competent maintains. Maintains isn't the word they put in the posting.

I'm still applying. I already know how this ends. I'm applying anyway, because the alternative is accepting the answer before I've asked the question.

I'm not ready to do that yet.
